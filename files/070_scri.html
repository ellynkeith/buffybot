Goodbye, Iowa

(January 12, 2000)

Written by: Marti Noxon

Teaser

INT. GILES' APARTMENT - DAY

Picks up not long after the (next to) last scene of Episode 13 ended. Buffy, Giles, Xander, Willow, Spike, and Anya are discussing Buffy's announcement that Sunnydale's no longer safe for any of them.

BUFFY
(mid-explanation)
...so Maggie sends me down into
the sewers with one of those
blasto guns. Next thing I know,
it's raining monsters.

XANDER
Hallelujah.
(off their looks)
What? She was asking for it.

BUFFY
(ignoring)
And this gate slams down behind
me... and I try to use the gun,
but it goes, pffft!

GILES
You're saying Maggie set you up?

BUFFY
That's what I'm saying. She sent
me on a one-way recon.

The gang absorbs the fact that Maggie is of the bad. Then -

SPIKE
Got to hand it to you, Goldilocks,
you have bleedin' tragic taste
in men. I have a cousin, married
a regurgitating Frovlax demon, has
better instincts than you.

BUFFY
What does my taste in men have to
do with this?

SPIKE
You think Riley was off knitting
booties for your future offspring
while Maggie was stringing you up?

The others look on guiltily. They'd had the same thought. But this stops Buffy. She hadn't considered it.

BUFFY
Riley? You think that he had
something to do with this?

GILES
Probably not. But we'd be remiss
if we didn't think through all the
possibilities.

BUFFY
(shaken)
Right... Remiss.
(then)
I - no... I mean, no. Maggie
made sure he was nowhere around
when she handed out the very
special "make Buffy dead"
assignment.

WILLOW
Plus, Riley - he seems like he
wouldn't tell a white lie, let
alone a whole lot of big, fat
dirty ones.

XANDER
That's why they call them "secret"
forces, Will. 'Cause they kind of
keep the whole lying thing to
themselves.

BUFFY
(back on topic)
All I know is that Maggie has it
in for me. And that means The
Initiative has it in for me.

WILLOW
Scary, on so many levels.

XANDER
Right. I'm guessing the mad
scientist isn't too keen on the
fact that the entire Scooby Gang
knows The Initiative is up to no
good.

BUFFY
Getting us back to the "not safe
for any of us" concept.

GILES
I still don't understand, Buffy.
What happened that would make
Maggie want to kill you?

BUFFY
I don't know. She didn't dig the
fact that I was asking a lot of
questions, that's for sure.

ANYA
So - you were getting too close to
something.

GILES
(gravely)
Clearly. I can only imagine what she
was so desperate to hide.

EXT. WOODS - DAY

A quiet, misty clearing in the woods. Then, in the middle of it, a steel door SLAMS OPEN, violently displacing the dirt and grass that covered it. Out of this secret opening appears ADAM, Maggie's experiment and killer, who crawls out and gains his feet awkwardly.

He lumbers forward - taking in the world around him with rapt fascination.

Then he moves into the woods, which is shrouded in a low-hanging fog, until he disappears into the distance...

BLACK OUT.

END OF TEASER

Act One

INT. GILES' APARTMENT - DAY

Buffy is gathering weapons from Giles' cabinet.

BUFFY
Everybody grab a weapon. We've
got to move.

XANDER
(pumping himself up)
And storm the Initiative? Yeah...
Let's take on those suckers!

BUFFY
I was more thinking that we'd hide.

XANDER
(relaxes)
Oh thank god.

BUFFY
We need to relocate to someplace
we're less likely to be found.
Come up with a plan.

WILLOW
We could go to my house.

BUFFY
(shakes her head)
The Initiative guys know how close
we are. They'll automatically
check the places you hang out.
(then)
Xander - what about your basement?
The guys haven't seen us together
much. And your basement's big
enough -

WILLOW
Plus - mirror ball.

XANDER
Cool. Come on down and boogie at
Xander's hide-away.

ANYA
(unenthusiastic)
Yes. Come boogie.

GILES
Absolutely not. I will not squat
in that dank little hole.

SPIKE
What? It was good enough for me,
but you're above it all?

GILES
Precisely. Besides, I don't see
why we can't stay right where we
are. I doubt very much that those
Initiative boys are going to come
around here looking for --

As if in answer, Riley enters.

RILEY
Buffy.

Buffy sees him and they move together, extremely relieved and glad to see each other. Any doubts or questions they have are clearly overshadowed by their need for each other.

RILEY (cont'd)
God, Buffy. Are you okay? What
happened?

BUFFY
You know?

RILEY
I know something went down. Tell me.

BUFFY
Maggie - she tried to kill me

Riley tries to process this. It's difficult. As he does:

ANYA
It didn't work, but they're all
upset anyway.

RILEY
Okay. Listen. I need you to go over
everything. Step by step. There must
be some kind of mistake -

XANDER
(hard)
There's no mistake. And how do
you know something happened?
Where were you?

Riley's spinning, trying to piece it all together.

RILEY
I was on a mission, but I came
back and... I - I'm not sure.
Let's just, keep our heads. Not
jump to any -

But he stops, his attention drawn across the room.

BUFFY
What?

Buffy follows his gaze. Sees that he's looking at SPIKE, who has also tensed.

RILEY
That's Hostile 17.

SPIKE
(bad American accent)
No, I'm just a friend of Xander's...
(drop accent)
Bugger it. I'm your guy.

Riley pushes back from Buffy. Even more confused.

BUFFY
That's - his name is Spike. It's
a... really long story. But he's
not bad anymore.

SPIKE
Hey! What am I? A bleedin'
broken record? I'm bad.
(to Riley)
It's just, I can't bite anymore,
thanks to you wankers.

RILEY
We've been looking all over the
place and you've known where he
was all along?

BUFFY
It's not like that.

RILEY
Then what is it like? What's he
doing here?

SPIKE
(heads for the door)
Leaving you swabs to your
dramatics, thanks. Got my stories
on the telly for that.
(to Riley)
By the by, if you are trying to
kill her --

He gives Riley TWO THUMBS UP, bails.

RILEY
(spun)
Buffy - what is this? You're hiding
an HST?

Xander, fuming now, stands - gets in Riley's face a little.

XANDER
Maybe you should back off and let
her ask the questions, Jack. Your
boss just tried to make monster
food out of her.

Riley stops, realizes that everyone, save Buffy, is looking at him a little accusingly.

RILEY
I didn't see much. I wasn't there...
(to Buffy)
All I know is, Professor Walsh
told me you were dead. But then
I saw you on the monitors...

Riley's clearly starting to flood. This is all too much to digest at once.

RILEY (cont'd)
This isn't Professor Walsh. There
must be something making her act
this way. Something, I don't
know, controlling her...

And it keeps coming, too fast -

GILES
We think Buffy was becoming too
inquisitive. That she may have
been getting close to something
Dr. Walsh was trying to hide. Any
idea what that might be?

BUFFY
What about 314? Maybe that's it -

RILEY
(to Buffy)
Maybe she was trying to test you?
What if it was only a drill -

BUFFY
Then why did she tell you I was
dead? Riley - this was no test -

GILES
I've heard rumors. That The
Initiative isn't what we've been
told. That, secretly, they are
working toward some darker
purpose... Something that might
harm us all -

RILEY
No. That's - that's not what
happens there.

BUFFY
Riley -

She reaches for him, but he pulls back - reeling.

RILEY
You have no right... I mean, I
think I would know if...

BUFFY
Nobody's sure of anything yet.
We're just trying to sort it out -

Riley starts to head for the door.

RILEY
I - I can't be here. I'll sort it out on
my own.

BUFFY
Riley -

RILEY
No. Just - I'm sorry.

And he goes.

OFF BUFFY

Looking after him, also feeing confused and lost.

EXT. WOODS - DUSK

Adam comes to the edge of the woods - sees a house in a clearing and a BOY (7 or 8) playing with his toys in the back yard. (We should note that he's playing with a cyborg type action figure that isn't entirely unlike Adam himself.)

Adam, intrigued with this small person, moves closer. The boy looks up - sees Adam. Adam draws back, afraid the kid's going to scream. But the boy doesn't - instead he drops his toy, comes forward, as interested in Adam as Adam is in him. A beat - then Adam speaks.

ADAM
What am I?

BOY
You're a monster.

ADAM
(takes this in)
I thought so...
(then)
What are you?

BOY
Me? I'm a boy.

ADAM
How do you work?

BOY
(stupid question)
I don't know. I just do.

Then the boy notices Adam's arm with the skewer in it. Is even more interested.

BOY (cont'd)
What's this for?

Adam considers telling him. Or showing him.

OFF THE KID

His innocent face staring up at Adam.

EXT. CAMPUS - NIGHT

It's late and the campus is fairly deserted. Riley walks alone, thinking. Trying to puzzle out what happened with Maggie and Buff. Trying to figure out what he's going to do...

INT. INITIATIVE - ROOM 314 - NIGHT

Dr. Angleman enters the darkened room. He tries to turn on the light, but nothing happens when he flicks the switch.

DR. ANGLEMAN
Dr. Walsh?

Nothing. He moves deeper into the room. Alarmed, he sees that ADAM has escaped his restraints and is not on the table. Then he looks to a far wall, sees that a security panel has been ripped off the wall and a number of electrical wires hang torn from it.

DR. ANGLEMAN (cont'd)
Adam?

Again. Nothing. Terrified, he starts to back out of the room - but SLIPS and falls on the floor.

CLOSE ON ANGLEMAN

As he sees that he's slipped in BLOOD.

DR. ANGLEMAN (cont'd)
Oh, God -

He scrambles desperately to get back to his feet. That's when he sees -

ANGLEMAN'S POV

Maggie's dead body lying face down (double anyone?) on the ground. She's been brutally skewered through the back.

BACK ON ANGLEMAN

Completely horrified.

INT. XANDER'S BASEMENT - NEXT DAY

Morning breaks, and our gang has spent a restless night in Xander's basement. We sees that the room has been DIVIDED BY A BLANKET THAT HANGS ON A ROPE into a section for the boys and a section for the girls (a la' "It Happened One Night")

On the boy's side we see Giles, looking intensely uncomfortable. He's clearly slept with a blanket on a plastic blow-up chair. On the Girl's side, we see Buffy, Anya, and Willow, still under the covers on Xander's bed - watching A ROADRUNNER CARTOON (a wonderful WARNER BROTHER'S cartoon!) Buffy, worried and distracted, is clearly not enjoying the animated diversion.

ON TELEVISION

The Roadrunner DROPS AN ANVIL ON THE COYOTE from about 10,000 feet. Coyote turns into an accordion guy, walks away.

BACK ON BUFFY AND THE GANG

BUFFY
(in all seriousness)
That would never happen.

WILLOW
Well, no, Buff. That's why they
call them cartoons and not
documentaries...

Giles' head pops up from the other side of the blanket.

GILES
Must we have the noise? My head
is splitting.

Willow sighs - turns the TV off.

WILLOW
Look who's a cranky bear in the
morning.

GILES
Yes. Can't imagine why I didn't
sleep well in my beachball.

ANYA
Every time you moved you made
squeaky noises. It was irritating.

GILES
Really? I'm surprised you could
hear it over your Wagnerian
snoring.

Buffy, upset by the arguing, interrupts.

BUFFY
Could we - not? Everything's
screwed up enough without you two
doing scenes from my parents' marriage.

Anya and Giles quiet, somewhat chastised.

ANYA
Sorry.

BUFFY
Thank you.

WILLOW
(to Buffy)
It'll be okay. Riley's just confused,
that's all.

BUFFY
I don't know. Seems like things
could get heavier. I mean, his
world's falling apart.

ANYA
And after you went through all that
stuff with Angel...
(then)
You should get a boring boyfriend,
like Xander.
(beat)
But you can't have Xander.

BUFFY
That was the idea. Riley was
supposed to be Mr. Joe Guy. We
were supposed to do dumb stuff -
like hold hands through the daisies
going, "tra, la, la..."

WILLOW
(sincere)
Poor Buffy. Your life resists all
things average.

ANYA
So dump him. But you can't have
Xander.

BUFFY
I'll try to remember that. Anyway,
it's too late. I'm already at the "I hurt
when he hurts, I smile when he smiles" stage.

ANYA
I hate that part.

Then a look of determination crosses Buffy's features.

BUFFY
So - I just have to make it work.

WILLOW
(encouraging)
I bet you will.

BUFFY
No. No bet. I will make it work.

This came out a tad more strident than she planned.

Now XANDER comes down the stairs with a tray full of breakfast-y items. He speaks urgently -

XANDER
Turn on the TV. Now.

Willow turns it back on. Xander puts the tray down.

CLOSE ON TELEVISION

Where a news reporter stands at a crime scene. THE WOODS NEAR THE BOY'S HOUSE.

REPORTER
... Sunnydale is still reeling from
news of the crime. A source in
the Coroner's Office tells us that
the boy was stabbed by what looks
like some kind of large skewer,
and his body was then mutilated.
Police have not named a suspect,
and the killer is still at large...

BACK ON GANG

As Xander turns the television off again. The mood in the room is grim.

BUFFY
The Polgara demon had a skewer in
his arm. That was the one Maggie
specifically wanted us to bring
back alive.

GILES
She must have set it after you.

BUFFY
But it got distracted. Oh, God . . .

WILLOW
Buffy. It's not your fault. How
could you know?

GILES
She's right. You mustn't blame
yourself.

PUSH IN ON BUFFY

As she climbs off the bed, in battle mode. It's classic General Buffy waging war against evil...

BUFFY
I'm not going to. I'm going to
the crime scene and see what I can
find out. You guys research the
Polgara demon - I want to know
where it is. And when I find
it - I'm going to make him pay for
taking that kid's life. I'll make
him die in ways he can't even imagine.

A beat. Then Buffy looks down at herself. Falters.

WIDEN ON BUFFY

As she looks at her outfit. She's wearing snuggly, adorable TEDDY BEAR PAJAMAS.

BUFFY (cont'd)
That... probably would have
sounded more commanding if I
wasn't wearing my Yummy Sushi
pajamas.

INT. LOWELL HOUSE - DAY

Riley is heading for his room, looking bleary-eyed. Forrest intercepts him.

FORREST
Hey - where you been all night?
(off his silence)
Well, congratulations. I see you
and Buffy have finally gotten past
the shy phase.

RILEY
I wasn't with Buffy. I needed to
be alone. Think things through.

FORREST
What things?

Riley considers his answer carefully. Looks down the busy hall. Opens his room door and gestures for Forrest to enter.

INT. RILEY'S ROOM - DAY

FORREST
This is mighty ominous. What's
up, man?

Riley closes his door - turns to him.

RILEY
Professor Walsh tried to have
Buffy killed.

FORREST
What?
(then/skeptical)
Did Buffy tell you that? I mean,
do you have any proof?

RILEY
I saw enough to know it's true.

Forrest doesn't know what to make of this. Then -

FORREST
I don't get it. Why?

RILEY
Buffy thinks that she was getting
too close to something. That
Professor Walsh has some secret -

FORREST
(considers/then)
I wouldn't put it past Buffy to
get on Professor Walsh's bad side.
She tends to put her nose where it
doesn't belong.

Riley is totally taken aback by Forrest's attitude. This is not what he was expecting.

RILEY
What?

FORREST
She's a pain, always wanting to
know why this, and why that -

RILEY
And you're saying she should die
because of that?

FORREST
I don't know. Maybe Professor
Walsh found out that Buffy was up
to something bad. That ever cross
your mind?

RILEY
(angered)
Why does it bug you so much that
I'm hanging with her? Is it
'cause she's a better soldier
than you?

Forrest also starts to burn.

FORREST
It bugs me that she's using you to
infiltrate our operation.

RILEY
You're saying she's a spy or
something? You're crazy.

FORREST
Riley, think about it! The
Professor's not stupid. If she
tried to kill Buffy - maybe Buffy
needed killing.

Riley, appalled, barely has time to react before a knock announces GRAHAM, who steps inside. He looks stricken.

GRAHAM
Guys...

RILEY
(sternly)
Not now, private.

They look and see Graham's obvious upset. Stop.

FORREST
What is it?

GRAHAM
Professor Walsh is dead.

OFF RILEY AND FORREST

Completely blown away.

BLACK OUT.


END OF ACT ONE

Act Two

INT. INITIATIVE - ROOM 314- DAY

Riley pushes his way through a crowd that is gathered around the door to room 314. Arrives and reacts to what he sees -

RILEY'S POV

The room is now cordoned off as a military crime scene. Maggie's lifeless - face down - body splayed on the floor. It looks as if she has been skewered (or staked) through the back by something.

BACK ON RILEY

Who takes this in, uncomprehending, as Forrest comes up behind him. Forrest gets one look at Maggie's wound, and his look of disbelief hardens into rage.

FORREST
Look at that wound. She's been
staked, wouldn't you say brother?

RILEY
What?

FORREST
Only one person I can think of who
could do something like that.

RILEY
You better not be saying what I
think you're saying. We don't
know a person did this. A Polgara
demon has -

FORREST
No way, man. That's your girlfriend's M.O.

Riley grabs Forrest hard by his jacket, yanks him close.

RILEY
Serious accusation. You better be
ready to deal with the
consequences -

Forrest PUSHES Riley off him. Hard.

FORREST
Bring 'em on. That supernatural
freak is blinding you and I'm sick of it!

RILEY
That's enough -

Riley barrels back at him, ready to trade blows. But they are stopped as DR. ANGLEMAN steps between them.

DR. ANGLEMAN
Stand back, men. Show some respect.

They manage to restrain themselves. Although it's clearly a struggle and they continue to stare each other down. (Also, from this point on - it should be noted that, occasionally, Riley scratches his right hand. It's not a big deal. No C.U.'s... Just, a small tic.)

DR. ANGLEMAN (cont'd)
(softer)
Listen. Everybody's upset. But
arguing isn't going to help
anything - and it's certainly not
what Professor Walsh would want.

He waits for a response from Forrest and Riley.

RILEY
No sir.

DR. ANGLEMAN
Alright, good. Now, Washington is
sending in a team to do an
internal investigation. I've been
told we have to wait for
word -

RILEY
What do you mean - wait? This has
to be the work of the Polgara
demon we captured last week -

DR. ANGLEMAN
Probably. It looks like, last night,
the Polgara escaped through
tunnel 72.

RILEY
It's out loose somewhere?

DR. ANGLEMAN
I'm afraid so.

RILEY
Then we have to go after it.

DR. ANGLEMAN
My orders from Washington are for
a total lock-down until they
arrive. I'm sorry.
(then)
Now return to your quarters.
There's nothing you can do here.

Angeleman moves off. Riley watches him until he is well out of ear shot, seething. Then he turns to Graham and the other assembled men.

RILEY
Listen. Angelman can talk all he
wants - but I'm still in charge
until the brass gets here and tells
me otherwise. And I say
we've got a demon to hunt.
(firmly)
Now suit up for armed patrol. And
by that I mean loaded guns, men.
Target practice is over - we're
going for blood.

The others follow his orders without hesitation. All except Forrest, who remains behind - glaring at Riley. Then, slowly, he moves off to join the rest of the men.

OFF RILEY

His expression set with dark rage.

EXT. WOODS - DAY

Buffy stands at the scene where the boy was killed. Two medical examiners roll a stretcher with a small covered body past her. Buffy watches, pained. She walks the circumference of the crime scene and bends to look at some footprints when she spots RILEY arriving. Glad to see him, wanting to make things right, she moves to him.

BUFFY
Hey.

RILEY
Hey.

BUFFY
Look - I'm sorry about earlier.
I know everyone came on pretty
strong, and the Spike thing - it
isn't as totally tweaked as it looks.
(off his silence)
Okay - maybe it is - but there's an
explanation that almost makes sense...

Riley's face is hard. Cold. He's barely looking at her.

BUFFY (cont'd)
Hello? I'm apologizing here. And
I think that's pretty big of me
considering I'm the one who almost
got made a demon sandwich. This
is the part where you throw me a
bone.

RILEY
(hard)
Maggie's dead.

Buffy takes this in, stunned by the news.

RILEY (cont'd)
You happy now?

Now she's appalled by Riley's coldness. A horrible beat, then -

BUFFY
Happy? How can you say that? Of
course I'm not. What happened?
Who killed her?

RILEY
That's classified.

BUFFY
Classified? Riley -
(holding it together)
The Polgara. It got her - then it escaped.
(nothing from him)
Didn't it?

Riley finally nods. Buffy can't contain her hurt at his behavior.

BUFFY (cont'd)
I'm going to find it. I'll find it and
destroy it. Then you'll stop asking
how "happy" all this death makes me.

She turns and walks away. Once she does, Riley's facade cracks and it's clear that he's conflicted - feels bad about hurting her.

INT. TARA'S ROOM - DAY

Tara responds to a knock at the door. It's Willow. Tara reacts with happy surprise.

WILLOW
Howdy.

TARA
I just got your message a minute
ago - I was in class. But I was
about to call you.

Tara stands back, welcomes Willow in.

WILLOW
I had so much fun the other night.
Those spells...

TARA
Yeah, that was nice.

WILLOW
(awkward)
I hope you don't think I just come
over for the spells and
everything. I mean, I really like
just talking and hanging out with
you and stuff.

TARA
I know that.
(then/smiling)
But you want to do a spell.

WILLOW
(sheepishly)
Yeah. But only because it's really
important. There's this -

TARA
(gently interrupting)
You don't have to explain. I don't
mind. Really.
(shyly)
I've been thinking about that last
spell we did all day.

WILLOW
(pleased)
You have? Oh.
(then/excited)
This one should be fun too. We
conjure the Goddess Thespia to
help us locate the demonic energy
in the area. Shouldn't be too tricky.

Tara looks a little uncertain at this.

TARA
The Goddess Thespia? Are you sure
we're ready for that?

WILLOW
(mock scoffing)
You and me? This is beneath us.

Tara takes this in. Finally smiles.

TARA
Okay. If you say so.

EXT. GRAVEYARD - DAY

A squad of Initiative guys sweeps the graveyard, heading into various crypts two at a time. Forrest and Graham, itching for the kill, are among them. They enter a crypt.

INT. CRYPT - DAY

Forrest and Graham look around the crypt. To their surprise it's lit with candles and sparsely furnished with an old arm chair, a rickety TV tray and a television that's hooked up to its own little generator.

GRAHAM
What do you think? A homeless guy?

FORREST
Could be. Or a squatter of the
demon variety.

GRAHAM
But not the Polgara.

FORREST
Who cares. I see a demon, it dies.

Graham feels the top of the TV.

GRAHAM
Warm.

Forrest NODS to the sarcophagus that dominates the room. They drag the lid off it.

CLOSE ON INTERIOR OF SARCOPHAGUS

Nothing but a female skeleton in a Victorian dress inside. They turn away from the sarcophagus.

FORREST
(frustrated)
Damn!

As he says it a frustrated Forrest shatters the TV screen with the butt of his gun.

FORREST (cont'd)
Animals...

They exit. A long moment passes, then -

CLOSE ON INTERIOR OF SARCOPHAGUS

Where we see SPIKE emerge from under the poofy skirts of the victorian dress the corpse wears (he's turned so that his feet are under her head, and his head lies under his legs...) He pulls himself out of the sarcophagus, looking a little shaken. That was a close call.

INT. WILLY'S BAR - DAY

The dingy bar looks a little different than the last time we saw it. The place is still a hole - but there have been small attempts at improvements; a green neon sign on one wall reads WILLY'S PLACE, a snazzy pinball machine sits in one corner. The bar is doing fair business - a gnarled demon sits huddled at a back table. A vampire sits with another demon at the bar...

Then there are a few low-life humans mixed into the bunch. Everybody pretty much minding their own business. BUFFY enters, finds Willy behind the bar. He's understandably less than happy to see her.

He gestures for her to move with him to a dark end of the bar - away from the customers. He glances around nervously.

WILLY
Aw man. You're killing me here.

BUFFY
I missed you too. Joint's jumping.

WILLY
Yeah, you know... I'm making some
changes with my life. Getting
away from my old image.

BUFFY
What, you mean, as a double
dealing snitch?

WILLY
Uh huh. I know you're gonna think
I'm blowing smoke - but after
those apocalypse demons nearly did
me in, I had an experience of the
spiritual variety -

Buffy doesn't have any time for this nonsense.

BUFFY
That's swell, really, but I still
need to know if you've heard
anything about a Polgara demon
doing some killing over the last
few days.

WILLY
See - that's the thing. I don't
talk behind people's backs no more.
(indicates the sign)
And I'm trying to give the joint
some class. It's Willy's "place"
now, see? Brings in a better
clientele.

Buffy glances around the room a little dubiously.

WILLY (cont'd)
And I got a deep fryer. These
demons just go crazy for chicken
fingers.
(desperate)
If they see me dealing with you -
then I'm just the same old Willy,
working both sides of the street.

Buffy's just about had it. Threatens -

BUFFY
